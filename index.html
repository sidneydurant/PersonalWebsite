Projects

SelfieAware is artificial intelligence put to good use. It was built at LAHacks by me and three friends. We implemented a facial recognition API, and used it to give people selfiness scores. To use SelfieAware, you have to login through Instagram, and then SelfieAware will automatically browse your recent Instagram pictures (no, not all 3,000 pictures we know you have) and give you a selfiness score based on the ratio of pictures of yourself vs pictures of nature/others/etc.

Queueup [q-up] is a party playlist maker. Built at MHacks Spring 2014 in 36 hours by me and two friends, Queueup lets you add songs to a central Spotify playlist. If you throw a party, all you have to do is setup a new playlist in the Spotify client app, and set up a playlist code. Partygoers can then simply enter the playlist code on the website to add music and upvote it. Go to the website and type in the code "MHACKS" to see the playlist we made at MHacks.

Samplify was an app built in Spring 2014. It used the Gracenote API to recognize a song over the microphone, and then scraped data from whosampled.com and use that data to identify what songs were sampled to make the one being listened to. We posted on reddit in the r/music subreddit, and ended up on front page. Samplify quickly reached the top 25 music-apps list in the Play Store before we were forced to take it down by whosampled.
